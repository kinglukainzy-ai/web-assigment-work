Philosophy & Wisdom

Black and white chains

Destiny and karma play a splendid game of chess,
Their laughter echoing through my storms of deep distress,
My joys and plights employed as nothing but their pawns,
And yet I stand here waiting, longing just to reach the dawn.

Ask not of me who I desire to claim the crown,
For if karma triumphs, every debt I owe he’ll hunt me down,
Let’s not call destiny either, for should she seize the lead,
I become a grain of sand lost in her ever-shifting creed,
Can I ever break free? I always ponder this with dread.

Tighter and tighter the endless duel begins to spread,
Both have lost their queen to each other's ruthless hold,
Neither king is safe, for one checkmate leaves them cold.
Let my smile not fool you, mortals,
For my dreams and hope I watch slip like water.

Firmly to the world I voice my insignificance,
Still yearning for the chance to rise and make my stance.
A wondrous game, you all call it with carefree glee,
But have you ever paused to think of me?
